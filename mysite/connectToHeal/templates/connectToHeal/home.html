Five-Minute Activities for Self-Compassion

We are all burdened with stress. However, taking a step back and prioritizing self-care is crucial for optimal health. While your day-to-day responsibilities are important, taking some time off them to reflect and refresh is equally necessary. Even if you pause and reflect for 5 minutes, you will notice changes in your mood, efficiency, and concentration. Here are a few 5-minute self-care activities that you can practice:

your Mental Load

Having too many things on your to-do list may overwhelm your mind. Spend 5 minutes a day thinking about the activities you can scrap from your day’s or week’s plate. Analyze if you can postpone your vacation plan or project deadline by a day or two. The more you lighten your mental load, the more pleasant you would feel without the extra weights bowing your mind down.

Breathe out your stress

Deep breathing is the easiest way to cultivate mindfulness. It helps to quieten your racing mind, improve your mood, and reduce your stress and worries. Breathing exercises like box-breathing or diaphragmatic breathing can help you relax in just 5 minutes. Moreover, deep breathing can be done anytime, anywhere, be it a meeting or a phone call.

Tune in to your favorite songs

Music goes a great way in energizing you and uplifting your mood. If there are songs that make you feel better, compile them in a playlist and use them for self-care. Tune in to the playlist for 5 minutes and dance with the released endorphins.

Scribble your nagging thoughts

Take out 5 minutes from your day to record your thoughts in a diary or journal. This would help you increase your awareness and relax. Carry your journal with you to school or the workplace and write down how you feel. This will help you reflect on your thoughts.

Get walking

Physical activity, no matter how trivial it may be, can do wonders for your mental health. Take a short 5-minute walk outside in your hallway. It will help you feel lighter and stimulate your thinking.

Create a ‘Gratitude’ list

If negative thoughts weigh you down, let a gratitude list help you climb. Create a list of things you are grateful for and focus on them. This can feel soothing, promote happiness, and instantly improve your mood.

Make room for self-compassion

Don’t be too hard on yourself. Don’t let your inner voice judge or criticize you. Pause for 5 minutes, check in on yourself and validate how you are feeling. Engage in kind self-talk and handle yourself compassionately.
